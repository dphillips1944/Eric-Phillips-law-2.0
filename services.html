Practice Areas

Focused, practical representation for some of life’s most serious legal challenges.

Criminal Law

When you’re facing criminal charges, experience matters. Eric F. Phillips brings more than two decades of legal experience to your defense, including time as a former prosecutor. He understands how cases are built, where weaknesses can be found, and how to protect your rights and future.

He represents clients in matters ranging from DUI cases to serious felony charges, including homicide. Criminal law consultations are free and may be scheduled in person, by Zoom, or by telephone.

Family Law

Family law includes divorce, custody, visitation, child support, modifications, paternity matters, guardianships, and enforcement actions. These cases affect your home life, finances, and relationship with your children.

Beth Lempp-Fields focuses almost entirely on family law and helps clients navigate these matters with clarity, preparation, and compassion. Consultations with Beth are available for $100.

Bankruptcy

Financial hardship can happen to anyone. Bankruptcy is often an important legal tool that gives people the opportunity to regain control of their future. We help clients understand whether Chapter 7, Chapter 13, or another approach fits their needs.

If you are considering bankruptcy, Eric handles these matters personally and offers straightforward advice in a confidential setting.

Stalking & Protection Orders

Protection order cases move quickly and can have serious consequences. If you have been accused, it is critical to understand your rights and prepare your response carefully. If you are seeking protection, experienced legal guidance can also make a meaningful difference.

Eric represents clients in these matters throughout Wyoming with professionalism, discretion, and careful preparation.

Wills & Trusts

A solid estate plan helps protect your loved ones and ensures your wishes are carried out. We help clients understand the differences between wills and trusts and choose the best path based on their goals, assets, and family needs.

Planning now can spare your family conflict, confusion, delay, and unnecessary expense later.
